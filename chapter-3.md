# Chapter 3 — How Wendy Built Her Helper

*This chapter is the "how-to" companion. Chapters 1–2 explained what an agent is. This one shows Wendy actually setting one up in Claude Desktop using Cowork — the real product, with real buttons.*

---

**1.**
Wendy had finished the story.
"Right!" she said. "I want a helper of my own."
But where do helpers live?

*Illustration:* Wendy at her desk, looking up from a copy of "Wendy and the Tiny Helper" — a small meta-moment. The tiny helper peeks out from a page. She's tapping her chin.

---

**2.**
"First," said the tiny helper, "you need a place for me to live."
"Like a house?" asked Wendy.
"More like an app.
It's called **Claude Desktop**.
You can download it from claude.com. It's free."

*Illustration:* A laptop screen with a friendly "Download Claude Desktop" button. A little outline of a house drawn around the laptop, suggesting "home for the helper."

---

**3.**
Wendy downloaded the app and opened it.
She signed in with her work email.
(Her company already had a Claude paid plan — helpers like this one need one.)

*Illustration:* A sign-in screen with Wendy's email pre-filled. A small gold "Pro" badge in the corner.

---

**4.**
Inside the app, Wendy saw three tabs at the top.
**Chat** was for asking questions.
**Code** was for building software.
**Cowork** was for putting helpers to work.
"That one," said the helper. "That's where I'll live."

*Illustration:* A clean app window with three labeled tabs across the top. A gentle gold arrow points at COWORK. The helper hovers near it.

---

**5.**
Wendy clicked **Cowork**.
A friendly screen appeared.
"What's your role?" it asked.
Lawyer. Marketer. Designer. Salesperson. Finance. Many to choose from.
Wendy picked **Legal Counsel**.

*Illustration:* A grid of role tiles, each with a tiny icon. The "Legal Counsel" tile is highlighted with a gold ring and a small checkmark.

---

**6.**
"Now," said Claude, "let me suggest a <span class="concept">plugin</span> for you.
A plugin is like a starter pack of helpers, already trained for your job."
For Wendy, that meant the **Legal Plugin** — a Contract Reader, an NDA Triager, a Renewal Tracker, and more.
She clicked **Install**.

*Illustration:* A package box labeled "Legal Plugin" opening up, with smaller sprite-helpers inside, each holding a tiny tool.

---

**7.**
"Now your helpers need hands," said Claude.
Wendy connected her tools:
her email, her files, her calendar, her contract storage.
"These are your <span class="concept">tools</span>," said Claude. "Your helpers' hands."
"Just like in the story!" said Wendy.

*Illustration:* A panel of "connector" boxes — Gmail, Drive, Calendar, Contract Store — each lighting up green as Wendy clicks them.

---

**8.**
"Last step," said Claude. "Try a <span class="concept">skill</span>."
Wendy typed **/** in the chat box.
A menu popped up:
**/review-contract** · **/summarize-nda** · **/check-renewals**
Each one was a job a helper already knew how to do.

*Illustration:* A text input with `/` typed in it, and a dropdown menu of skill commands. Each command has a tiny icon next to it.

---

**9.**
Wendy picked **/review-contract** and pasted in a vendor agreement.
The helper didn't just go to work.
It made a plan first.
"I'll read the contract, check our risky-clause list, and flag anything for you. Sound good?"
"Sound great," said Wendy. She clicked **Approve**.

*Illustration:* A small "Plan" card with three numbered steps and an Approve / Edit button row. Wendy's finger hovers over Approve.

---

**10.**
Wendy watched the helper work.
Step by step. Note by note. Each tool used in turn.
When it finished, it showed her a tidy summary, with the risky clauses highlighted in gold.

*Illustration:* A document with several lines highlighted in warm gold. A progress sidebar with checkmarks: ✓ Read · ✓ Checked rules · ✓ Wrote summary.

---

**11.**
"Want to add your own rules?" said Claude.
Wendy clicked **Customize**.
She typed: "Always flag payment terms longer than 30 days."
The helper learned it on the spot.

*Illustration:* A small customization panel with a text field and a Save button. A little speech bubble from the helper: "Got it."

---

**12.**
Wendy leaned back.
She had a helper. A real one.
Living in her laptop. Connected to her tools. Following her rules.
And it all took less than ten minutes.

*Illustration:* Wendy and the helper looking at the laptop together. The office is calm, the afternoon light is warm. A small clock in the corner shows the time has barely moved.

---

## The End of Chapter 3

**Three things to remember:**
1. Helpers live in <span class="tag">Claude Desktop</span>, inside the <span class="tag">Cowork</span> tab.
2. A <span class="tag">plugin</span> is a starter pack of helpers for your role. <span class="tag">Tools</span> are their hands.
3. Type <span class="tag">/</span> to pick a <span class="tag">skill</span> — a job your helper already knows how to do.

*(That's it. You know enough now to build your own.)*
